# Senior Partner & Business Skills Review (v3.0)

**Date:** March 12, 2026

**Attendees:** Senior Partner (IB), Chief Business Officer, Chief of Staff

**Objective:** Assess the Vanta Signal prototype (v3.0) from a strategic, commercial, and investment perspective to evaluate its optionality and define the go-forward roadmap.

---

## 1. Investment Banker's Assessment: A Platform, Not a Product

The asset has evolved beyond a "prototype" and is now a functional, multi-channel intelligence platform. The current state represents a significant de-risking of the technical and product vision. From an M&A and strategic investment perspective, the key value drivers are:

- **Proprietary Data & Infrastructure:** The native phone integration (FMC) is the most defensible asset. Owning the network and the Call Detail Record (CDR) is a structural moat that cannot be replicated by software-only competitors.
- **Extensible Architecture:** The channel-based architecture (iMessage, Gmail, Zoom, Phone) is designed for expansion. This is not a single-product company; it is a platform for building new intelligence applications.
- **Embedded AI:** The classification pipeline (Gemini) is a core competency, not a feature. The ability to extract structured data from unstructured communication is the fundamental value proposition.

**Valuation Lens:** At this stage, the asset would be valued as a technology platform, not on its current revenue (which is zero). The valuation would be a multiple of the addressable market for communication intelligence, discounted for execution risk. The key question an investor would ask is: "How big is the market for a system that knows what you said, who you said it to, and what it means?"

## 2. Chief Business Officer's Mandate: From Prototype to Product

The platform is technically impressive, but it is not yet a product. A product is a repeatable, scalable, and marketable solution to a customer problem. To bridge this gap, the CBO issues the following directives:

- **Define the Customer & Use Case:** Who is the first customer for this platform? Is it the creative entrepreneur? The enterprise sales team? The investment firm? We must define a specific, beachhead market and build a solution tailored to their needs.
- **Productize the Platform:** The current dashboard is a powerful internal tool. The next step is to package a subset of its functionality into a commercial offering. This could be a "Pre-Meeting Brief" subscription, a "Relationship Intelligence" service, or a "Deal Flow" tracker.
- **Establish a Commercial Model:** How do we charge for this? Per seat? Per signal? Per channel? A freemium model with a paid enterprise tier? We need to define a pricing strategy that aligns with the value delivered.

**Roadmap Directive:** The immediate roadmap should focus on productization. This means moving from a technology-centric view ("Look at all the signals we can capture!") to a customer-centric view ("Here is a specific problem we can solve for you.").

## 3. Chief of Staff's Strategic Optionality Framework

The current platform creates multiple strategic options for Vanta Wireless. The role of the Chief of Staff is to preserve and enhance this optionality.

| Option | Description | Path to Value | Key Dependencies |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **1. Internal Tool** | Keep Vanta Signal as a proprietary, internal intelligence tool to give the executive team an unfair advantage. | Enhanced decision-making, faster deal cycles, stronger network effects. | Continued internal development, tight access control. |
| **2. Standalone SaaS Product** | Spin out Vanta Signal as a new, standalone SaaS company targeting a specific vertical (e.g., sales teams, VCs). | High-margin recurring revenue, potential for high valuation multiple. | External funding, dedicated management team, full go-to-market buildout. |
| **3. Feature of Vanta Wireless** | Bundle Vanta Signal as a premium feature for Vanta Wireless MVNO customers. | Increased ARPU, lower churn, strong differentiator vs. other carriers. | Deep integration with Vanta Wireless billing and provisioning systems. |
| **4. M&A / Acqui-hire Target** | Position the platform and the team for acquisition by a larger player (e.g., Salesforce, Microsoft, Zoom). | Strategic premium on valuation, accelerated path to market. | Strong IP portfolio, well-documented codebase, demonstrated traction in a target market. |

**Recommendation:** The optimal path is to pursue **Option 3 (Feature of Vanta Wireless)** in the near term, while preserving the potential for **Option 2 (Standalone SaaS Product)** in the long term. This means building the platform with a clear multi-tenancy architecture in mind, even if the first customer is Vanta's own user base.

## 4. Unified Strategic Recommendation

The consensus of the business review is as follows:

1.  **Prioritize the Stabilization Sprint:** The P0 engineering and product gaps must be closed immediately. A stable, reliable platform is the prerequisite for any commercialization effort.
2.  **Define the First Product:** The next development cycle should focus on defining and building the first commercial product offering, likely a "Pre-Meeting Brief" service delivered via the platform.
3.  **Architect for Multi-Tenancy:** The engineering team must build with a multi-tenant architecture from day one to preserve the option of a standalone SaaS product in the future.
4.  **Defer New Channel Integration:** The existing five channels provide more than enough data for the initial product. New channel integrations (e.g., Slack, Teams) should be deferred until the first product has achieved market fit.
